Television Energy Consumption

In Australia, all televisions must display energy rating labels. Modern LED and OLED TVs are far more energy-efficient compared to older plasma and LCD models — helping you save money while enjoying premium picture quality.

Why TV Energy Ratings Matter

The Australian Zoned Energy Rating Label (ZERL) for televisions shows estimated yearly energy consumption in kilowatt-hours (kWh). A 6-star TV can use up to 40% less energy than a 3-star model of the same size. Over a decade, that adds up to significant savings on your electricity bills.

Did you know? A 55-inch OLED TV with a 6-star rating costs roughly $25–35 per year to run, compared to $70–90 for an older 3-star LED model.

Smart Tips to Reduce TV Energy Use

  • Choose higher star ratings — Aim for 5 stars or more when buying a new TV
  • Reduce screen brightness — Lower brightness by 20-30% cuts energy use significantly
  • Turn off when not in use — Avoid standby mode; use a power strip to fully disconnect
  • Enable eco/power saving modes — Most modern TVs have built-in energy saver settings
  • Use sleep timers — Automatically turn off your TV if you fall asleep watching
